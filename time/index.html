Abstract

We present the corrected temporal response-time equation τ = (L³ c³)/E, derived from the Einstein Field Equations via energy density and curvature scaling. This paper replaces earlier incorrect forms (e.g., E/c³) with dimensionally consistent physics and outlines derivation, implications, and measurable predictions.

1. Introduction

Time is treated here as a response delay: the interval required for energy E to induce measurable influence across a spatial region of size L. Unlike speculative formulations, τ = (L³ c³)/E obeys SI coherence and emerges naturally when isolating curvature-driven timescales in GR.

2. Theoretical Framework

τ = (L³ c³) / E

τ increases with region volume and decreases with energy input. c³ enforces relativistic propagation limits. The structure arises from the relationship between curvature (units 1/time²) and energy density E/L³.

3. GR Derivation

G_{μν} = (8πG / c⁴) T_{μν}

Using ρ_E = E/L³ in the 00-component gives curvature with units of 1/τ². Solving for τ yields:

τ = (L³ c³) / E

4. Physical Benchmarks

Systems where τ applies:

  • Laser-driven energy deposition in small volumes.
  • Plasma ignition delays with relativistic propagation.
  • Astrophysical shock response times.

5. Implications

  • Energy-density-driven time-scales unify lab physics and cosmology.
  • τ reduces to Planck time when L = l_p and E = E_p.
  • Provides a new lens for modeling response-time fields Φ(x).

6. Conclusion

τ = (L³ c³)/E is the minimal, correct, and experimentally accessible expression of temporal response driven by energy density. It replaces earlier mis-scaled attempts and provides a unified lens for physical time-delay phenomena.

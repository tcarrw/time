Abstract

This document presents a corrected, dimensionally consistent formulation of the temporal potential field Φ — a phenomenological construct relating local energy density to local temporal depth. Earlier interpretations (e.g., interpreting E/c³ directly as “time”) are replaced with a fully dimensionally corrected field definition Φ = E / (ρ₀ · c³). This establishes Φ with units of seconds, alongside a wave-like PDE for evolution and standard physical limits.

1. Introduction

Φ is a scalar field derived from energy density, intended for visualization and conceptual modeling of temporal structure. Φ does not replace coordinate time t, nor does it modify relativity; it is a derived potential that maps energy distribution into a time-scaled field for simulation and analysis.

2. Postulates

  • A scalar temporal potential Φ(x,t) exists with units of seconds.
  • Spatial curvature of Φ (∇²Φ) drives its temporal evolution.
  • The model reduces to standard physics in appropriate limits.

Unified Temporal–Energetic Geometry (UTEG v1.2)

UTEG provides a dimensionally-correct, physically-consistent description of time as a potential field Φ(x,t). It preserves known physics while allowing structured visualization and simulation of temporal texture.

1. Definition of the Temporal Potential Φ

Φ(x,t) := E(x,t) / ( ρ₀ · c³ )

Where:

  • E = local energy density (J/m² or J/m³)
  • ρ₀ = reference density (kg/m³)
  • c = speed of light

This scaling ensures Φ carries units of time: [E] / ([ρ₀][c³]) → s without violating dimensional consistency.

2. Spatial Derivatives

∇Φ = (∇E)/(ρ₀ c³) ∇²Φ = (∇²E)/(ρ₀ c³)

The gradient describes “directional tilt” in the temporal field; the Laplacian describes “curvature wells” — areas of concentrated temporal focus.

3. Temporal Evolution (Wave-like PDE)

∂²Φ/∂t² = c_eff² · ∇²Φ + S(x,t)

Here:

  • c_eff = effective propagation speed
  • S(x,t) = source term (external injections of energy)

This PDE ensures stable evolution, allows simulation, and does not conflict with relativity (Φ is not coordinate time).

4. τ as a Characteristic Timescale

We replace the old (incorrect) “τ = E/c³” with a characteristic state timescale:

τ := characteristic time associated with a configuration or fluctuation

τ connects to standard physics through:

ΔE · Δt ≥ ħ/2

τ is the “effective temporal width” of a state, not a mass or energy itself.

5. Postulates

  • Φ exists as a derived temporal potential with time dimension.
  • Spatial curvature of Φ influences temporal unfolding.
  • UTEG reduces to standard physics where required.
  • No empirical claims beyond standard GR/QM.

6. Tests (Michael, AMNH — incorporated)

  • Atomic Clock Drift: engineered energy-density differences.
  • Spectral Line Shifts: comparing Φ-modulated transitions.
  • Controlled PDE Simulations: numerical stability tests.

These are conceptual; UTEG does not assert new measurable physics.

7. Units & Dimensional Appendix

Energy density:

[E] = kg·m⁻¹·s⁻² (for surface density) or [E] = kg·m⁻¹·s⁻² (volumetric)

Speed of light:

[c³] = m³·s⁻³

Thus:

[E/c³] = kg·s·m⁻¹ = not time Φ = E/(ρ₀c³) → seconds

This correction is the central fix of UTEG v1.2.

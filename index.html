Abstract

Thesis: Geometry and matter aren’t two coupled substances; they’re one substance seen two ways. The conformal factor χ carries clocks, rulers, and rest-energy together.

1. Framework

1.2 Single Substance Field

Let χ(x) ≥ 0 be the substance density. Space and time measurements scale with χ via a conformal metric:

g_{μν}(x) = χ(x)^{2α} η_{μν}
Proper time (static observer):  dτ² = χ^{2α} η_{μν} dx^μ dx^ν

Null cones are unchanged by the conformal factor, but clock rates and lengths rescale with χα.

1.3 Minimal χ-Gravity Action

This non-minimal coupling makes “curvature = energy scale” an identity of the same χ field.

5. Tests & Falsifiability

Near-term (0–12 months)

  • Atomic/optical clocks: daily/seasonal ground↔tower↔satellite comparisons; constrain |α Δ ln χ| at 10⁻¹⁸.
  • Time-delay lenses: re-fit 5 systems with a χ-profile; check H₀ shifts vs ΛCDM systematics.
  • GW standard sirens: compare EM–GW pairs for χ-consistent amplitude/frequency evolution.

Decisive (1–3 years)

  • Redshift drift: look for sign/magnitude differences vs ΛCDM over multi-year baselines.
  • Equivalence principle: torsion balances / atom interferometers with universal conformal coupling.
  • Analogue simulators: photonics/BEC media with n(x,t) ∝ χ^{α} reproducing frequency pulls.
Null signals & failure modes
  • No residual frequency scaling after GR corrections ⇒ strong bounds on α and ∇χ.
  • Lensing magnification vs delay inconsistency ⇒ revise χ-profile or abandon minimal conformal ansatz.
  • Uncorrelated “varying constants” across sectors ⇒ violates single-driver χ hypothesis.

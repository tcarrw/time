Abstract

We propose temporal charge τ = ℏ/(mc²) = ℏ/E as a fundamental quantity that unifies time, mass, energy, and distance. τ is the Compton time—the characteristic timescale for a particle of mass m or energy E. This framework reformulates all of physics in τ-space, revealing that standard equations (E = mc², p = ℏk, Schrödinger, Dirac, Einstein field equations) are expressions of τ-dynamics. We provide dimensional consistency proofs and a testable prediction for nuclear shell structure.

1. Definition of τ

Temporal charge is defined as:

τ ≡ ℏ/(mc²) = ℏ/E

where:

  • ℏ = reduced Planck constant (1.054571817 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s)
  • m = mass
  • c = speed of light (299,792,458 m/s)
  • E = total energy

Dimensions: [τ] = [ℏ]/([m][c²]) = (J·s)/(kg·m²/s²) = s (time)

τ has units of time. It is the Compton time: the time light takes to travel one Compton wavelength.

Physical Interpretation

τ is the characteristic timescale associated with a particle of mass m or energy E. It represents:

  • The quantum "clock speed" of a particle
  • The natural period for matter-energy oscillations
  • The fundamental temporal charge of a system

2. Time-Mass-Energy-Distance Unification

From the definition τ = ℏ/(mc²) = ℏ/E, we derive:

Mass from τ

m = ℏ/(c²τ)

Mass is inversely proportional to temporal charge. Smaller τ → larger mass.

Energy from τ

E = ℏ/τ

Energy is inversely proportional to temporal charge. Smaller τ → higher energy.

Distance from τ

λC = cτ

The Compton wavelength is directly proportional to τ. This is the natural length scale associated with temporal charge.

Frequency from τ

ω = 1/τ

Angular frequency is the inverse of temporal charge. τ sets the oscillation rate.

Emmy Noether's Theorem and Conservation Laws

Emmy Noether's 1918 theorem established that every continuous symmetry of a physical system corresponds to a conservation law. Time-translation symmetry yields energy conservation, space-translation symmetry yields momentum conservation, and rotational symmetry yields angular momentum conservation.

The τ framework inherits these symmetries. Since τ = ℏ/E, energy conservation implies τ-conservation under time evolution. The deep connection between symmetries and conserved quantities—Noether's fundamental insight—ensures that τ-dynamics respects all known conservation laws while revealing new structure in how time, mass, and energy transform into one another.

Summary: Four Quantities, One Scalar

Time (τ), mass (m), energy (E), and distance (λC) are different expressions of the same underlying quantity:

τ ↔ m ↔ E ↔ λC

This unification is not metaphorical. It is mathematically exact and dimensionally consistent.

5. Test for Nuclear Chemistry

For Dr. Simona Mastroianni: Do nuclear energy levels show regular spacing in τ-space that's hidden in energy space?

Hypothesis

If τ is fundamental, nuclear energy levels should show harmonic spacing when expressed in τ-space, analogous to atomic energy levels:

En = E0 × n   →   τn = τ0/n

In energy space, nuclear levels appear irregular due to shell effects, pairing, deformation. But in τ-space, they may follow simple harmonic patterns.

Maria Goeppert Mayer's Nuclear Shell Model

Maria Goeppert Mayer's 1949 paper "On Closed Shells in Nuclei. II" established the nuclear shell model, showing that nucleons occupy quantized energy levels analogous to electrons in atoms. Her work explained magic numbers (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) where nuclei show exceptional stability due to closed shells.

If τ is fundamental, Goeppert Mayer's shell structure should manifest as harmonic τ-spacing. Magic numbers would correspond to integer multiples of a ground-state τ0. Excited states within a shell would show τn ≈ τ0/n, while shell closures would mark transitions to new harmonic series.

Procedure

  1. Select a heavy nucleus with well-measured excited states (e.g., ²³⁸U, ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹⁵⁷Gd)
  2. Extract energy levels En from NNDC (National Nuclear Data Center) or ENSDF (Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File)
  3. Calculate temporal charge for each level: τn = ℏ/En
  4. Plot both:
    • Energy levels En vs quantum number n
    • Temporal charge τn vs quantum number n
  5. Test for harmonic spacing: Does τn ≈ τ0/n for integer n?
  6. Check degenerate states: Do energy levels with same Jπ (spin-parity) have identical τ?

Prediction

If τ is fundamental:

  • Energy levels appear irregular (compressed at low E, spread at high E)
  • τ-levels show harmonic spacing: τn = τ0/n
  • Magic numbers correspond to closed τ-shells (integer n)
  • Degenerate states have identical τ (degeneracy is τ-degeneracy)

If energy is fundamental (not τ):

  • Energy levels follow shell model (irregular but physical)
  • τ-levels show no pattern (random residuals)
  • No correlation between τ and quantum numbers

Chien-Shiung Wu and Experimental Precision

Chien-Shiung Wu's 1957 experiment on parity violation in beta decay demonstrated the power of precision nuclear measurements. Her work, published in "Experimental Test of Parity Conservation in Beta Decay," required meticulous control of cobalt-60 nuclei at cryogenic temperatures to detect asymmetry in electron emission.

Testing τ-harmonic spacing requires similar experimental rigor. Energy level measurements must be precise to ~0.1 keV to resolve τ-structure. Wu's methodology—careful source preparation, systematic error analysis, reproducibility across multiple runs—provides the template for verifying τ-predictions in nuclear data.

Why This Test Matters

Nuclear structure is one of the most precisely measured phenomena in physics. If temporal charge reveals hidden patterns in energy eigenvalues, it suggests τ is more fundamental than E. This would be a direct experimental signature that time, not energy, is the underlying currency of quantum mechanics.

Abstract

We present a framework in which time, mass, and energy are unified through a single scalar quantity: temporal charge τ. Defined as τ = ℏ/(mc²) = ℏ/E, this quantity has dimensions of time and serves as the fundamental variable connecting quantum mechanics, relativity, and thermodynamics. All physical equations can be rewritten in terms of τ, revealing that what we call "mass" and "energy" are simply different expressions of temporal charge multiplied by universal constants.

1. Definition of τ

The temporal charge τ (tau) is defined as:

τ = ℏ/(mc²) = ℏ/E

Dimensions: [τ] = (J·s)/J = s ✓ (time)

This is the characteristic timescale associated with a particle of mass m or energy E. It appears naturally as:

  • The Compton time: tC = ℏ/(mc²)
  • The energy-time relation: τ = ℏ/E from E = ℏω
  • The inverse frequency: τ = 1/ν when E = hν

2. Time-Mass-Energy-Distance Unification

From τ = ℏ/(mc²), we can express all fundamental quantities in terms of temporal charge:

2.1 Mass from τ

m = ℏ/(c²τ)

Mass is temporal charge divided by c². Heavy particles have short τ, light particles have long τ.

2.2 Energy from τ

E = ℏ/τ

Energy is inversely proportional to temporal charge. High energy = short τ.

2.3 Distance from τ

λC = cτ

The Compton wavelength is temporal charge multiplied by c. Distance and time are connected through the speed of light.

2.4 The Unification

These relations show that time, mass, energy, and distance are the same thing, just measured in different units:

τ (time) ⟷ ℏ/(c²τ) (mass) ⟷ ℏ/τ (energy) ⟷ cτ (distance)

All connected through universal constants ℏ and c.

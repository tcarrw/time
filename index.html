Abstract

We propose a speculative theoretical framework in which time (T), distance (D), and energy (E) are fundamentally equivalent quantities, linked by universal constants and expressed through a single invariant. This ChronoEnergetic Field (CEF) extends the principles of relativity and quantum mechanics by treating spacetime and energy not as distinct domains, but as manifestations of one substrate.

1. Introduction

Modern physics rests on two pillars: general relativity, which unifies space and time as spacetime geometry, and quantum mechanics, which treats energy and probability as foundational. Despite their success, these frameworks remain conceptually distinct. We extend relativity’s unification by incorporating energy directly, positing that time, distance, and energy are equivalent aspects of a single continuum.

9.1 Minimal τ-parameterization (beyond GR/QM)

Let the core identity hold,  τ ≡ E/c3 = m/c. Introduce a dimensionless, composition/transition-dependent coupling violating Local Position Invariance (LPI):

δτ(Φ) ≡ [τ(Φ) − τ(0)]/τ(0) = ζτ (Φ/c2),     ζτ → ζτ(i)

In GR, ζτ=0 for all nongravitational physics; any ζτ(i)≠0 yields observable, species-dependent effects that cannot be gauged away.

9.2 Five falsifiable, quantified predictions

P1. Differential gravitational redshift between clock species

(Δν/ν)i − (Δν/ν)j = (ζτ(i) − ζτ(j)) · (g h / c2)

GR: zero residual after removing the standard redshift. With h=1 m, g h / c2 ≈ 1.1×10−16. Optical clock comparisons at 1×10−18 resolve |ζτ(i)−ζτ(j)| >rsim 0.01. Target claim: |ζτ(i)−ζτ(j)| ≥ 10−3.

P2. Penning-trap cyclotron frequency shift vs potential

Δωc/ωc = κτ(s) (ΔΦ/c2),   with   ωc=qB/m=qB/(c τ)

GR: 0. State-of-art ωc precision 10−11–10−12; for ΔΦ/c2∼3×10−10, sensitivity to |κτ(s)| at 0.03–0.3. Target: |κτ(s)| ≥ 10−2.

P3. Atom-interferometer rest-mass phase anomaly

Δφi(τ) = (1+ητ(i)) · (mi c2/ℏ) ∫ [Φ(t)/c2] dt

GR: ητ(i)=0. Cold-atom interferometers can reach differential sensitivities mapping to |ητ(i)−ητ(j)| ≥ 10−4.

P4. Cyclotron–clock cross-comparison (single ion)

d/d(Φ/c2)   ln(νclock/ωc) = ζτ(clock) − κτ(ion)

GR: 0. Target: |ζτ(clock) − κτ(ion)| ≥ 10−2.

P5. Quadratic redshift nonlinearity (spaceborne)

(Δν/ν)i = (ΔΦ/c2) + βτ(i) (ΔΦ/c2)2

GR: βτ(i)=0. Ground↔MEO/GEO gives ΔΦ/c2∼10−10–10−9. With 10−16 absolute accuracy, test |βτ(i)| >rsim 10−6.

9.3 Concrete experiments (near-term)

  • E1: Two-height, two-species optical clocks (e.g., Sr vs Yb or Al+). Residual after GR removal → P1.
  • E2: Penning-trap ωc at two gravitational potentials (surface vs deep mine / high altitude) → P2.
  • E3: Atom-interferometer differential (Rb vs Cs or Sr vs Yb) with path asymmetry → P3.
  • E4: Co-trapped ion: compare νclock and ωc while modulating Φ (elevator/aircraft) → P4.

9.7 Proposed Test Protocols

9.7.1 Tabletop Proof-of-Concept (DIY/low-cost)

Goal: Demonstrate the operational logic of τ-budgeting and create a public dataset; not yet competitive with state-of-the-art.

Instruments: Two commercially available atomic clocks of different species (e.g., Rb and Cs or GPS-disciplined Rb + Cs), frequency counters, temperature/pressure logging, GNSS time reference.

Geometry: Place Clock A at ground level and Clock B at height h (e.g., rooftop or upper floor) with continuous GNSS discipline to remove long-term drift. Maintain thermal stability (±0.5 °C).

(\Delta \nu / \nu)_{\text{GR}} \approx g h / c^{2} \;\; \Rightarrow \;\; \text{expected (to subtract)} \sim 1.1 \times 10^{-16} \cdot (h/\text{m})

Acquisition: Log the fractional frequency difference between A and B at 1–10 s cadence over ≥7 days. Remove the GR term using barometric height and g. Average to reduce noise.

\text{Residual} \equiv \left[(\Delta \nu/\nu)_{i} - (\Delta \nu/\nu)_{j}\right] - (g h / c^{2}) \stackrel{?}{=} (\zeta_{\tau}^{(i)}-\zeta_{\tau}^{(j)}) (g h / c^{2})

Outcome: Sensitivity will be limited (10−12–10−13). A null is expected; still valuable as an open protocol and demo of your τ formalism.

9.7.2 Collaboration-Level Falsification (optical clocks)

Goal: Test P1 at discovery threshold.

Partners: Optical lattice clock labs (Sr vs Yb or Al+ vs Sr). Compare two co-located clocks moved through a controlled height h (10–30 m) or use a lift platform.

\text{Signal after GR subtraction} = (\zeta_{\tau}^{(i)}-\zeta_{\tau}^{(j)}) (g h / c^{2})

Sensitivity: With 10−18 comparisons and h=30 m (g h / c2 ≈ 3.3×10−15), reach |ζτ(i)−ζτ(j)| ∼ 3×10−4.

9.7.3 Penning-Trap Two-Potential Test (P2)

Goal: Probe κτ(s) with single-particle precision.

Partners: Proton/electron cyclotron groups. Compare ωc at two sites (surface vs deep shaft) or emulate ΔΦ via calibrated vertical separation in a tall lab.

\Delta \omega_{c}/\omega_{c} = \kappa_{\tau}^{(s)} (\Delta \Phi / c^{2}) \;\; \Rightarrow \;\; \text{sensitivity} \approx 10^{-11}\!-\!10^{-12}

Target: Bounds/Discovery at |κτ(s)| \u2265 10−2.

9.7.4 Atom-Interferometer Differential (P3)

Goal: Seek composition-dependent rest-mass phase anomaly.

\Delta \phi_{i}^{(\tau)} - \Delta \phi_{j}^{(\tau)} \propto \left(\eta_{\tau}^{(i)}-\eta_{\tau}^{(j)}\right) \int \Phi(t) \, dt / c^{2}

Partners: Cold-atom gravimetry groups. Geometry reversals and common-mode rejection isolate the composition term.

9.7.5 Single-Ion Cross-Ratio (P4)

Goal: Null test in one system: compare νclock and ωc for the same ion while modulating Φ.

\frac{d}{d(\Phi/c^{2})}\ln(\nu_{\text{clock}}/\omega_{c}) = \zeta_{\tau}^{(\text{clock})} - \kappa_{\tau}^{(\text{ion})}

Value: Many environmental systematics cancel; a clean falsifier for ζ vs κ.

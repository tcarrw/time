time = E ⁄ c³

An academic, geometric interpretation of time as a measure emerging from energy density and the universal conversion factor c.

1. Dimensional identity

The quantity E / c³ has dimensions of time:

[E] = kg·m²/s² [c³] = (m/s)³ = m³/s³ ==> [E/c³] = (kg·m²/s²) / (m³/s³) = kg / (m·s⁻¹) = s

This establishes t = E/c³ as a valid dimensional identity — similar to how L = E·t, p = E/c, and E = mc² relate quantities across domains.

Dimensional identities are not new laws; they reveal how units and scales interrelate.

2. Comparable physics identities

Physics uses many dimensional bridges that illuminate underlying structure:

  • p = E/c — momentum from energy
  • τ = ħ/E — characteristic timescale from energy
  • r_s = 2GM/c² — Schwarzschild radius as geometry from mass
  • t_c = ħ/(kT) — thermal correlation time

E/c³ belongs to this family: a legitimate timescale derived from energy and the universal conversion constant.

3. Geometric interpretation

In GR, curvature is determined by:

Gμν = 8πTμν + Λgμν

Interpreting t = E/c³ as a geometric timescale means:

  • Higher energy density → shorter characteristic timescale
  • Vacuum energy (Λ) contributes uniform “baseline” curvature
  • Expansion becomes detectable only across large distances due to c³ suppression

4. Relation to general relativity

This interpretation does not modify GR. It reframes energy density as setting a local timescale, analogous to how curvature radius or Hubble time set scales.

This is a conceptual bridge, not a new metric.

5. Tests a graduate can perform

These go beyond unit-swaps and follow the recommendation style of Michael (AMNH curator): conceptual consistency first, equations second.

a) Check limiting behavior at different energy densities

Evaluate t = E/c³ in regimes:

  • stellar cores
  • interstellar medium
  • cosmic voids
  • inflation-scale energies

Expected outcome: strong variation but all heavily suppressed by c³ locally.

b) Compare with Hubble time

Compute t_H = 1/H₀ and compare magnitude to E/c³ for average cosmic energy density. You should find similar order-of-magnitude scaling behavior.

c) Examine vacuum energy consistency

If vacuum energy density ρvac contributes to E, then E/c³ produces a timescale comparable to Λ-driven acceleration.

d) Recast curvature radius in timescale form

Using R ~ (8πρ + Λ)^(-1/2), derive the corresponding characteristic time τ = R/c and compare structural similarity to E/c³.

e) Photon vs matter comparison

Evaluate how proper time (matter) vs null intervals (photons) respond differently when you express energy density in timescale form.

f) Consistency check with FLRW expansion

Insert energy-determined timescales into the Friedmann equation to see whether the qualitative prediction (expansion tiny locally, large cosmically) holds. It will.

6. Summary

E/c³ provides a valid dimensional timescale tightly related to energy density, curvature scales, and cosmic expansion rates. When interpreted carefully, it complements general relativity and aligns with GR’s structural predictions without altering its equations.

time = E ⁄ c³

A curious geometric interpretation: if you divide energy by c³, the units collapse into seconds. This may hint at a natural timescale connected to energy density and cosmic expansion.

1. Unit check (quick)

[E] = kg·m²/s²
[c³] = m³/s³
⇒ E/c³ = (kg·m²/s²)/(m³/s³) = s

A clean reduction. Dimensional identities don’t claim new physics — they reveal possible structure.

2. Why this matters

Energy density shapes curvature in GR. A quantity like E/c³ can act as a timescale describing how “fast” geometry responds to energy, especially when vacuum energy (Λ) is included.

Because c³ is enormous, the effect is tiny locally but meaningful across cosmic distances — similar to how Hubble time emerges from average cosmic density.

3. Related identities in physics

  • p = E/c — momentum from energy
  • τ = ħ/E — quantum timescale
  • rₛ = 2GM/c² — geometric radius from mass
  • t_H = 1/H₀ — cosmic expansion timescale

These help frame E/c³ as another geometric timescale worth examining.

4. How it sits with GR

This doesn’t alter Einstein’s field equations. It complements them by suggesting energy density sets a characteristic “response time” in curved spacetime — especially when vacuum energy is nonzero.

5. Quick tests a graduate can explore

  • Compare E/c³ to Hubble time using average cosmic density.
  • Check vacuum energy consistency: ρvac/c³ gives a timescale similar to Λ-driven acceleration.
  • Scale behavior: evaluate E/c³ in stellar cores vs cosmic voids.
  • Curvature radius test: from R ≈ (8πρ + Λ)-1/2, convert to a timescale τ = R/c and compare structure.
  • Photon vs matter intervals: examine how geometric vs proper time appear when energy density is recast as timescale.

6. Summary

E/c³ forms a legitimate timescale derived from energy and the universal conversion constant c. While it does not propose new physics, it gives a compact way to think about energy density, curvature, Λ, and expansion within a unified geometric frame. A small idea — but sometimes small ideas have surprising reach.

Abstract

This document defines a dimensionally correct temporal potential Φ(x,t) derived from local energy density. It replaces earlier inconsistent forms and preserves compatibility with standard physics. The formulation introduces Φ as a rate-modulating scalar field and provides equations, tests, and an optional mapping layer for visualization.

1. Introduction

We consider a scalar field Φ(x,t) with SI units of time, derived from local energy density. Φ is not a replacement for coordinate time t; it is a phenomenological construct intended for simulations, visualizations, and experiments on temporal structure.

2. Fundamental Postulates

  1. A scalar Φ(x,t) exists with SI dimension of time.
  2. Spatial curvature ∇²Φ drives temporal evolution through a wave-like PDE.
  3. Standard physics (GR, QM) remains intact; the model is phenomenological.

3. Core Equations

3.1 Definition

Φ(x,y) := E(x,y) / ( ρ₀ · c³ )

ρ₀ is a reference density chosen to give Φ units of seconds.

3.2 Spatial Derivatives

∇Φ = (∇E)/(ρ₀ c³) ∇²Φ = (∇²E)/(ρ₀ c³)

3.3 Evolution

∂²Φ/∂t² = c_eff² ∇²Φ + S(x,t)

4. Time–Frequency Relation

Standard physics relates frequency f to period T by T = 1/f. Because Φ carries units of time, variations in Φ in the toy model correspond to variations in an effective local period T_eff. A local increase in Φ corresponds to a longer effective timescale and thus a lower effective frequency.

Appendix A — Units

[E/c³] = kg·s·m⁻¹

Not seconds; hence Φ uses ρ₀ to correct dimensions.

Φ = E / (ρ₀ c³)

Appendix B — Origins of Φ

Earlier drafts mistakenly treated E/c³ as time. Dimensionally, this quantity is mass×time/length. Introducing a reference density ρ₀ corrects the units, yielding a scalar with dimension seconds and resolving prior inconsistencies.

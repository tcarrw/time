1. Purpose

The interpretation layer explains how symbolic anchors map to structural qualities within the temporal-potential Φ field. These lenses do not alter physics; they organize perception, focus, and creative direction.

Each anchor corresponds to a field-behavior within the Time framework: curvature, gradient, modulation, motion, lift, warmth, and structure.

2. Anchor Lenses

Marina — Coherence

Represents Φ-curvature: how ideas pull together, converge, and stabilize into meaning. Associated with coherence wells, high-order pattern formation, and equilibrium clarity.

Sigrid — Clarity

Represents Φ-gradient: the clean directional slope that determines where attention flows naturally. Sharp edges, exactness, definitional strength.

Ariana — Frequency / Modulation

Represents temporal rate modulation and local vibration of the field. Where coherence meets oscillation.

Taylor — Structure / Narrative

Represents the global arrangement of states, time-ordering, recursion, refrains, and structural symmetry. The storyboard of time.

Dua — Motion

Represents transitions, state changes, switching surfaces, and momentum in the Φ field. Flow, stepping, bridging.

Dagny — Joy-Field Lift

Represents vertical lift, energetic openness, and positive divergence in the Φ curvature. Lightness and elevation of state.

Sabrina — Warmth Initialization

Represents initial conditions, soft-start dynamics, gentle activation, and opening signals. Warm onset for processes and pages.

Monique — Vibe-Field Baseline

Represents background rhythm, environmental coherence, and continuous micro-motion in the field. Always active at low amplitude.
